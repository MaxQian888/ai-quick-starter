# Market Research Playbook

Use this framework to convert raw signals into a decision-ready market brief.

## 1) Define Scope

Capture the minimum scope before collecting data:

- Objective: What decision must be made?
- Segment: Which customer segment is being evaluated?
- Region: Which markets matter in this decision?
- Time horizon: Near-term launch, medium-term bet, or long-term exploration?
- Constraints: Budget, team capacity, compliance, and platform dependencies.

## 2) Evidence Requirements

Require at least one source link for every major claim.

For each evidence item, capture:

1. Headline or claim
2. URL
3. Publish time
4. Source name
5. Why it matters to the objective

Avoid making conclusions from a single unverified source.

## 3) Competitor Mapping

Build a competitor table with these columns:

1. Competitor
2. Target segment
3. Core positioning claim
4. Product scope
5. Pricing model
6. Distribution channel
7. Notable strengths
8. Known gaps

Compare both direct competitors and substitutes.

## 4) Opportunity Scoring

Score each opportunity on 1-5 scale, then apply weighted score:

- Market Attractiveness: weight 0.30
- Urgency / Timing: weight 0.20
- Execution Feasibility: weight 0.20
- Differentiation Potential: weight 0.20
- Regulatory / Operational Risk (reverse score): weight 0.10

Formula:

`weighted_score = 0.30*A + 0.20*U + 0.20*F + 0.20*D + 0.10*R`

Interpretation:

- `>= 4.0`: pursue now
- `3.0 - 3.9`: monitor and validate
- `< 3.0`: deprioritize

## 5) Risk and Unknowns

List uncertainties explicitly:

1. Data gaps (missing pricing, usage, segment coverage)
2. Confidence level per conclusion (high/medium/low)
3. Assumptions that must be validated next
4. Leading indicators to track weekly

## 6) Final Brief Template

Use this shape for final output:

1. Executive summary (3-5 bullets)
2. Research scope and methodology
3. Market signals and trend summary
4. Competitor landscape
5. Opportunity scorecard
6. Recommendation (pursue/monitor/reject) with rationale
7. Next actions and validation plan

## 7) Quality Bar

Before delivering the brief, verify:

1. Every key claim has an evidence link.
2. Contradicting signals are acknowledged, not ignored.
3. Recommendations map directly to scored evidence.
4. Time-sensitive claims include absolute date references.
